Rules of Algebraic Identification

I. Foundational Definitions

1. The Factor

Definition: A component that is linked to others strictly by multiplication.

  • Criteria: It must multiply the entire numerator or denominator.
  • Behavior: Can be "unhooked" and cancelled individually.

2. The Term

Definition: A component that is linked to others by addition or subtraction.

  • Criteria: Separated by \(+\) or \(-\) signs.
  • Behavior: Glued to the unit. Cannot be cancelled on its own.

II. Criteria for Cancellation

Cancellation is the division of the numerator and denominator by the same number. It only works if the number is a "Factor."

The Three Cancellation Criteria:
  1. The number must be a Factor (multiplied by the whole top/bottom).
  2. The numerator and denominator factors must be an Exact Match.
  3. If a component is inside a sum (e.g., \(x+2\)), the entire bracket is the factor.
Positive Example (Valid): $$\frac{3(x+y)}{3}$$ Why: The \(3\) is a factor multiplying the whole bracket. Criterion met.
Result: \(x+y\)
Negative Example (Invalid): $$\frac{3+x+y}{3}$$ Why Not: The \(3\) on top is a term. It is not multiplying the top. Criterion failed.
Result: Cannot be simplified.
Positive Example (Unit Match): $$\frac{(x+2)(x-3)}{(x+2)}$$ Why: The entire bracket \((x+2)\) is a factor on both top and bottom.
Result: \(x-3\)
Negative Example (Breaking Units): $$\frac{x+2}{x}$$ Why Not: You cannot cancel the \(x\). It is a term inside the locked unit \((x+2)\).
Result: Cannot be simplified.

III. Criteria for using LCM

LCM (Least Common Multiple) is a "bridge" builder used to combine two separate fractions into one.

The Three LCM Criteria:
  1. The operation must be Addition \(+\) or Subtraction \(-\).
  2. There must be two or more separate fraction bars.
  3. The denominators must be different.
Positive Example (Need LCM): $$\frac{x}{2} + \frac{y}{3}$$ Why: Addition between different denominators.
Action: Find LCM (6). Result: \(\frac{3x+2y}{6}\)
Negative Example (No LCM): $$\frac{x}{2} \cdot \frac{y}{3}$$ Why Not: This is multiplication. LCM is never used for multiplication.
Action: Multiply across. Result: \(\frac{xy}{6}\)

IV. The Algebraic Cheat Sheet

Association: \((a+b)+c = a+(b+c)\) — Grouping doesn't change a sum.
Commutation: \(a+b = b+a\) — Order doesn't change a sum.
Distribution: \(a(b+c) = ab+ac\) — Outer factor visits every inner term.
The Unit Rule: If you see \(x+y\), treat it as a single unit \((x+y)\).
